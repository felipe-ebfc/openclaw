# AEC Workflows with EBFC AI

This guide shows you how to work EBFC AI into your daily construction routine. Each workflow is a 3-step process you can repeat every day.

The goal isn't to change how you work — it's to cut the time you spend on documentation, reporting, and prep so you can stay focused on the job.

---

## Morning Routine: Start the Day Sharp

**What it's for:** Get oriented fast before you hit the field or your first meeting.

**Step 1 — Ask for your project status:**
> "What are the open action items on Riverside Commons from this week?"

**Step 2 — Review yesterday's loose ends:**
> "What did I flag yesterday that still needs follow-up?"

**Step 3 — Set your focus:**
> "I need to focus on the elevator shaft conflict today. What do I know about it?"

Osito pulls from everything you've shared before — project context, past meetings, flagged issues — and gives you a summary you can use to walk into your morning meeting ready.

**Power tip:** Do this while you're having coffee before the crew shows up. 5 minutes with Osito beats 20 minutes digging through emails.

---

## RFI Drafting: From Field Issue to Formatted RFI

**What it's for:** Turn a field conflict or drawing question into a ready-to-submit RFI in under 5 minutes.

**Step 1 — Describe the issue:**
> "I've got a conflict on the Level 5 slab. The structural drawings show a beam at elevation +58'-6" but the MEP drawings have a 16-inch duct running through the same space. Grid line D-4."

**Step 2 — Tell Osito what you need:**
> "Draft an RFI to the structural engineer asking for clarification and a proposed resolution."

**Step 3 — Review and adjust:**
> "Add a reference to spec section 03 30 00 and note that we need a response within 5 business days."

Osito formats a proper RFI with project name, description of conflict, reference drawings, and requested response. You copy it into your RFI log or project management system.

**Power tip:** Keep a running list. "Log this as RFI issue #47 for Riverside Commons." Osito tracks it and can reference it later.

---

## After a Meeting: Minutes in Minutes

**What it's for:** Turn your meeting notes — even rough ones — into formatted minutes with action items.

**Step 1 — Share what happened:**
> "OAC meeting today, March 7. Attendees: Felipe (EBFC), Sarah (Skyline Dev), Tom (Turner GC), Jenny (MEP). Here are my notes: [paste your raw notes, voice memo transcript, or summary]"

**Step 2 — Ask for the output:**
> "Format this as meeting minutes and pull out all action items with owners and due dates."

**Step 3 — Refine if needed:**
> "Add a 'Decisions Made' section" or "Tom's last name is Martinez."

Osito produces minutes that look like they took 30 minutes to write. They took 2.

**Power tip:** You don't need perfect notes. Even messy bullet points work. "Felipe to follow up on curtain wall submittal, due Friday. Tom said the concrete pour is back on schedule. Sarah wants updated schedule by EOW." That's enough.

## End of Day: Daily Report

**What it's for:** Get your daily report done before you leave the site — not at 9pm from the kitchen table.

**Step 1 — Tell Osito what happened:**
> "Daily report for Riverside Commons, March 7. Crew: 12 ironworkers, 4 carpenters, 2 operators. Work completed: set steel at floors 8 and 9, east elevation. Issues: one crane delay, 2 hours lost to wind hold from 10am to noon. Weather: overcast, 42 degrees, winds 22 mph."

**Step 2 — Add anything notable:**
> "Also note: Subcontractor Rodriguez Concrete confirmed they'll mobilize Monday for the Level 10 deck pour."

**Step 3 — Get the report:**
> "Write the daily report."

Osito formats a clean daily report with all the right sections. Copy it into your project management system or email it to the owner.

**Power tip:** Do this in the truck before you drive off. Voice-to-text in Google Chat + Osito = daily report done before you hit the highway.

## Weekly PPC Review: Percent Plan Complete Analysis

**What it's for:** Prepare for your pull planning or production meeting with variance analysis and talking points.

**Step 1 — Share your numbers:**
> "PPC review for week ending March 7. We planned 18 tasks, completed 13. That's 72%. Reasons for misses: 2 tasks blocked by late submittal, 1 weather delay, 2 crew availability."

**Step 2 — Ask for analysis:**
> "What's my variance breakdown by category? And what should I flag in the meeting?"

**Step 3 — Prepare talking points:**
> "Give me 3 talking points for the pull planning session based on this week's misses."

Osito gives you a clear variance breakdown and helps you walk into the meeting with a plan, not just numbers.

**Power tip:** Track it week over week. "How does this week's 72% compare to the last 3 weeks?" Osito remembers if you've been logging consistently.

## Power User Tips

**Reference past context.** Once you've loaded a project and had a few sessions, Osito remembers. "What was the last RFI we drafted for Riverside Commons?" works.

**Be the editor, not the writer.** Osito drafts — you refine. A 90% draft you edit in 2 minutes beats writing from scratch every time.

**Use it for the stuff you procrastinate on.** Daily reports. Meeting minutes. RFIs. The documents you know you need but keep putting off. That's where EBFC AI pays off fastest.

**Ask follow-up questions.** If the output isn't right, keep talking. "Make it more detailed." "Shorter." "Add the submittal log reference." "Formal tone." Osito adjusts.

**Log issues as they happen.** Don't wait until the end of the day. If something comes up at 2pm, message Osito: "Log this: found rebar missing at column C-8, notified Tom at Turner, waiting on response." It goes into memory and you can pull it up anytime.

**Talk to it like a colleague, not a search engine.** Don't type keywords. Type sentences. "What do I know about the electrical rough-in schedule for Riverside Commons?" beats "electrical schedule Riverside."
