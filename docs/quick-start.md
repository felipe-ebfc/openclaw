# Getting Started with EBFC AI

Welcome. You've got a lot on your plate — submittals due, subs to chase, RFIs stacking up, and a meeting in an hour. EBFC AI is here to take some of that off your hands.

This guide gets you up and running in about 15 minutes.

---

## What Is EBFC AI?

EBFC AI is your construction companion — an AI assistant that **remembers your projects** and gets smarter the more you use it.

Most AI tools are like talking to a stranger every single time. EBFC AI is different. It remembers:

- Your projects, contracts, and key contacts
- RFIs you've drafted and issues you've flagged
- Action items from your meetings
- Your preferences for how things get done

You talk to it through **Google Chat** — the same app you probably already use with your team. Just message Osito (your AI companion) like you'd message a colleague.

---

## Your First 3 Things

### 1. Set Up Your Profile

Tell Osito who you are. This takes 2 minutes and makes everything smarter from day one.

Just send this message in Google Chat:

> "Hey Osito — I'm [Your Name], [Your Role] at [Your Company]. I'm working on [Project Name], a [brief description, e.g., 'ground-up 4-story mixed-use in Austin']. My main contacts on the job are [GC/Owner name]."

Osito will store this and use it in everything you do going forward.

---

### 2. Load Your First Project

Tell Osito the basics about a project you're actively working on. The more it knows, the more useful it is.

Example:

> "Load this project into your memory: Project name is Riverside Commons. It's a 12-story multifamily in Denver, 220 units. Owner is Skyline Development. GC is Turner. We're in structural steel phase, target completion October 2026. Current open issues: curtain wall submittal late, elevator shaft conflict on floors 8-9."

After this, Osito knows your project — and you can reference it by name in future conversations.

---

### 3. Try Your First Task

Pick something off your to-do list and let Osito take a first pass. The easiest starting points:

**Draft an RFI:**
> "Draft an RFI for a conflict I found between the structural drawings and the MEP drawings at the Level 3 mechanical room. The issue is the ductwork path interferes with a steel beam at grid line C-7."

**Summarize meeting notes:**
> "Here are my notes from today's OAC meeting: [paste or type your notes]. Pull out the action items and format this as meeting minutes."

You'll have a clean draft in seconds. Edit it, copy it, send it — done.

---

## Talking to Osito

No special commands. No syntax to learn. Just talk to Osito in plain English, the way you'd explain something to a smart colleague.

**Be specific.** The more context you give, the better the output.

- Instead of: "Write an RFI"
- Try: "Write an RFI about the concrete mix spec on the parking deck — the spec calls for 4000 PSI but the structural engineer's notes reference 5000 PSI. We need clarification from the EOR."

**Mention project names.** Once a project is loaded, you can reference it naturally.

> "For Riverside Commons, what were the action items from the last meeting?"

**Ask follow-ups.** If the first draft isn't quite right, just say so.

> "Make it more formal" or "Add a section for proposed resolution" or "Shorter."

## What Osito Can Help With

### RFI Generator
Describe a field conflict, drawing discrepancy, or spec question — Osito drafts a properly formatted RFI ready to submit.

### Meeting Minutes
Paste or describe what happened in a meeting. Osito formats it as minutes and pulls out action items with owners and due dates.

### Daily Report
Tell Osito what happened on the job today — crew counts, work completed, issues encountered. It writes your daily report.

### PPC Assistant (Percent Plan Complete)
Share your weekly PPC numbers and your plan. Osito helps you analyze variance, spot trends, and prepare for your pull planning session.

### Project Context
Load a project into Osito's memory so it knows your job inside and out. Reference it in any future conversation without re-explaining.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is my data private?**
Yes. Your information is stored in a private memory space that only you and EBFC can see. It is never used to train AI models or shared with other users.

**Can Osito send emails or submit RFIs for me?**
Not automatically. Osito drafts content for you — you copy, review, and send. You stay in control of what goes out.

**Does it work on my phone?**
Yes. Google Chat works on iOS and Android. You can message Osito from the job site, the truck, or anywhere you have signal.

**What if it gets something wrong?**
Just correct it. "That's not right — the spec section is 03 30 00, not 03 20 00." Osito will update and try again. The more you correct it, the better it gets at your preferences.

**Do I need to be a tech person to use this?**
No. If you can send a text message, you can use EBFC AI. There's nothing to install beyond Google Chat.
